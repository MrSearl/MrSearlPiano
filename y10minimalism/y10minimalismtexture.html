Texture - Minimalism

Texture is an important feature of minimalist music and is often created through the layering of repeated patterns. Pieces may begin with a thin texture before additional instruments and musical ideas are gradually introduced. As layers combine and overlap, the texture becomes thicker and more complex. These subtle changes help maintain interest while preserving the repetitive nature of the music.

Example:

A minimalist piece may begin with a single repeated pattern before gradually adding more layers, creating an increasingly rich and detailed texture.
