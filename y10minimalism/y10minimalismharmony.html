Harmony - Minimalism

Harmony in minimalist music is often simple and slow-moving. Rather than using complex chord progressions, minimalist composers frequently repeat a small number of chords or remain on the same harmony for long periods of time. This allows listeners to focus on the interaction between different layers and patterns. Small harmonic changes can have a powerful effect because they occur gradually and are often repeated many times.

Example:

A minimalist piece may repeat the same few chords throughout most of the music, with subtle harmonic changes creating a gradual sense of development and movement.
