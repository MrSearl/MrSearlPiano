Texture - House Music

Texture in house music is usually layered, repetitive, and gradually developed over time. Many house tracks begin with a thin texture using only drums or percussion before additional layers such as basslines, synths, chords, vocals, and effects are gradually introduced. House music often uses a combination of rhythmic and harmonic layers working together to create a full and energetic sound. The texture may become thicker during choruses or drops and thinner during breakdowns, helping to create contrast and maintain interest throughout the track.
