Task 5 - ‘Radetzky March' by Johann Strauss

Radetzky March, Op. 228, was written by the Austrian composer Johann Strauss in 1848. It quickly became popular and was often played by marching soldiers.

The piece was written to celebrate a military victory, but its mood is cheerful and festive rather than serious. Its strong beat and lively tempo make it easy to march and clap along to.

Johann Strauss was a composer of the Romantic period and is best known for his waltzes and marches. Today, Radetzky March remains one of his most famous works and is often performed by orchestras around the world.
