Task 3 - ‘Flight Of The Bumblebee ’ by Nikolai Andreyevich Rimsky-Korsakov

Flight of the Bumblebee was written by the Russian composer Nikolai Rimsky-Korsakov for his opera The Tale of Tsar Saltan around 1900. The music is designed to sound like a bumblebee flying quickly and changing direction.

The piece is famous for its extremely fast notes and lively tempo. It requires a great deal of skill because the performer must play many notes accurately at high speed.

Although the notes themselves are not unusually high or low, the speed and precision needed to play them make the piece very challenging. Today, Flight of the Bumblebee is one of the most well-known examples of virtuosic music.
