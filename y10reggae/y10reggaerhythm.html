Rhythm - Reggae Music

Rhythmic techniques are one of the most important features of reggae music. Reggae rhythms are usually relaxed and heavily syncopated, meaning that emphasis is often placed on weaker beats or offbeats rather than strong beats. A common rhythmic feature is the “One Drop” drum pattern, where the kick drum and snare drum emphasise beat 3 of the bar instead of beat 1. Guitar and keyboard chords are often played off the beat using short chord stabs known as the “skank,” helping to create the distinctive reggae groove. Repetition and steady pulse are also important in maintaining the consistent feel of the music.
